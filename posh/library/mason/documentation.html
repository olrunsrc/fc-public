Sheep / Dog Demo --- Scheduled POSH



This is an example of a BOD/MASON library built for scheduled POSH.  Scheduled POSH is based on SoL (Bryson & Thórisson 1999). 

This library has not been used for any experiments, just for developing BOD/MASON.  It has been described a little by Bryson, Caulfield and Drugowitsch (2006).  The library sheepDogDemo contains the SPOSH version of this demo, with some further extensions.  See for another example library latch Test, which has associated publications on action selection (Rohlfshagen & Bryson 2008).

This library was first developed by Tristain Caulfield for EASSS in July 2005.  Tristan was primarily trying to conect jyPOSH (by Andy Kwong) to MASON and never really worked with or understood BOD.  It was updated for the new versions of BOD/MASON by Jan Drugowitsch in 2006-7.   It is being improved for better behavior decomposition (and better behaviour!) by Joanna Bryson in 2008.

What does it do?

There are two types of agents distributed randomly around the field --- sheep and dogs.  In Tristan's version, the dogs just chase the sheep.  The sheep flock (see Reynolds 1987).  They also panic (see Aylett 199?), which means they move closer to each other when they either sense a predator or sense that the other sheep are paniced.

I am making a new version where both the sheep and the dogs flock, so it is more clear that different agents can share behaviors in the behavior library.  This will also make the dogs behave less stupidly.  The difference is that the dogs flock with both dogs and sheep, but don't panic and have a larger avoidance value so they generally wind up on the outside of the herd.  Meanwhile the sheep don't see the dogs as flock-mates, they see them as predators, so they panic.  Panic should be represented with a flexi-latch to demonstrate the emotion drive thing.  If I have time, I will also add a behaviour to the dogs where they chase strays.  There may be a drive to look for strays which would then really demo flexi-latch and the drive/emotion commonality.
